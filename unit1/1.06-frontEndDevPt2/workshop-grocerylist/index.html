FAQs

What is sinigang?

  • Sinigang is a Filipino sour and savory soup. The Tagalog people traditionally use unripe tamarind to flavor the broth. For this variation, we will make the soup with shrimp.

Ingredients for Sinigang Na Hipon (Filipino Sour Soup with Shrimp)

Produce

  • tomatoes
  • onions
  • ginger
any seasonal vegetables like the following:
  • okra
  • eggplant
  • long string beans
  • taro
  • daikon
  • water spinach
  • jalapeno

Pantry Items

  • rice (also to use for rice washing)
  • fish sauce (Rufina brand is great)
  • tamarind powder packet

Proteins

  • whole unpeeled shrimp

Where to Buy Ingredients

Pro-tip:

All ingredients can be procured in any market, except fish sauce and the tamarind powder. Please look for them in the Asian food aisle at your local market, or also go support your local Asian market!

Cooking Sinigang na Hipon for Community

This is a suggested recipe, measurements are not exact.
FishGlitch followed what their ancestors would have done-- make a big batch to share!

Ingredient Qty Preparation
water 16 quarts set aside for broth
rice water 1-2c from 2nd rice washing set aside for broth
fish sauce to taste set aside for broth
tamarind powder 1 packet; to taste set aside for broth
tomatoes six (6) pcs quartered
onions three (3) large pc quartered
ginger thumb-sized sliced
taro a handful peeled and roughly cut into 2" pcs
eggplant one large pc slice 1.5" thick pcs
long string beans one bunch snap by hand into 2" strips
okra two handfuls keep whole
daikon one pc peeled and thinly sliced
water spinach one bunch snap by hand to separate leaves and stalks (2" long)
whole shrimp 1 lbs rinsed

Instructions

  1. Wash rice and set aside the second rice washing. Cook rice in a rice cooker or pot.

  2. Wash and cut all vegetables.

  3. Bring water, rice washing, tomatoes, onions, and ginger to a boil until tomatoes and onions are soft.

  4. When broth is in a rolling boil, add in this order for thorough and delicate cooking:

    • taro
    • eggplant
    • jalapeno
    • shrimp
    • fish sauce and tamarind powder, to taste
    • long string beans
    • okra
    • daikon
    • water spinach

  5. Serve in bowls with sides of hot rice!

Pro tip: the veggies and shrimp can be removed from the boiling broth and set aside to serve, to they won't overcook in the pot.
